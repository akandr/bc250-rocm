=== Instrumented eviction profiling and wedge ordering (2026-07-14) ===
Board: BC-250, Fedora 43, Linux 6.18.9, stock amdgpu module, 40 CU,
amdgpu.sched_policy=2, THP=madvise. Native gfx1013 rocBLAS SGEMM reproducer
(sgemm_iter N iters). Method: ftrace function_profile / function tracer on the
KFD eviction path, with the reproducer's per-iteration stdout written into
trace_marker so iterations and eviction calls share one clock.

These are the captured tool outputs behind the quantitative claims in
Section "A Native gfx1013 rocBLAS, and the Liveness Wedge".

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
[E1] function_profile over a full sgemm_iter 1024 500 run (completed, correct)
     ($T/trace_stat, filtered to the eviction path)

  Function                               Hit    Time            Avg             s^2
  --------                               ---    ----            ---             ---
  kgd_hqd_destroy                          6    5334.487 us     889.081 us      33532.81 us
  svm_range_evict.isra.0                   2    3027.417 us     1513.708 us     4580804 us
  evict_process_queues_nocpsch             1    3024.072 us     3024.072 us     0.000 us
  restore_process_queues_nocpsch           1      85.899 us       85.899 us     0.000 us

  Reading: over a 500-GEMM run the process is evicted once or twice
  (evict_process_queues_nocpsch=1, svm_range_evict=2); each eviction preempts
  the process's ~3 queues (kgd_hqd_destroy 6 hits / ~2 evictions = 3 per
  eviction) at ~0.9 ms per queue on a HEALTHY queue; queues are restored
  ~0.09 ms later. Evictions are rare per-process events, not per-GEMM.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------
[E2] munmap count per process (strace -c -f -e trace=munmap), completed runs

     iters=5    munmap=266
     iters=25   munmap=266
     iters=105  munmap=266

  Reading: the runtime issues a CONSTANT ~266 munmap calls per process
  (init/teardown code-object and module management), independent of GEMM
  count. So evictions are driven by process-lifetime unmap activity, not a
  per-dispatch drumbeat.
